This Privacy Policy lays it all out. By using my website, you’re agreeing to the terms below. I’ve kept it simple and approachable—like we’re chatting about it over coffee. Let’s get started!

1. Information I Collect

When you stop by my website, some info might get collected. Here’s what I (or the tools I use) might pick up:

a. Personal Information

  • Stuff you give me directly: Right now, my site doesn’t have contact forms, comment sections, or anywhere for you to enter your name, email, or personal details. It’s just me sharing my Addons and bio, so no need to worry about handing over personal info. If I add features like that later (say, a feedback form), I’ll update this policy and give you a heads-up.

b. Non-Personal Information

  • Device info: I can see what you’re using to visit—like a phone, tablet, or computer—plus your browser (Chrome, Firefox, etc.), operating system (Windows, iOS, Android), and IP address.
  • How you use the site: With Google Analytics, I can track which pages you check out, how long you stick around, and what you click. For instance, I might see which Addon is a hit or if my bio page keeps you hooked.
  • Cookies: The site uses cookies (tiny files saved on your device) to keep tabs on things like your browsing habits or to help AdSense show ads that match your interests.

2. How I Collect Information

I don’t sit there gathering your info by hand—it’s all automatic through the tools I use. Here’s the breakdown:

  • Cookies and tracking tech: When you visit, cookies get stored on your device. Think of them as little reminders that help me (and Google) recognize you next time, while also powering AdSense ads and Analytics data.
  • Google Analytics: This tool tracks how you use the site, using cookies to log things like your time spent or favorite pages.
  • Google AdSense: To make a bit of revenue, I use AdSense for ads. It taps into cookies to figure out what you’re into (based on your browsing) and picks ads that fit.

Don’t like cookies? You can turn them off in your browser settings. Just know that some parts of the site might not work as smoothly if you do.

3. What I Use the Information For

I’m not collecting info just because—it’s all got a purpose. Here’s why:

  • Keep the site running smoothly: Device info helps me make sure the site looks good on any screen, from phones to desktops.
  • Make things better: Google Analytics shows me what’s popular—like which Addons you love or if my bio needs a boost.
  • Show relevant ads: AdSense uses the data to pick ads you might like. For example, if you’re into tech, you’ll see software ads, not furniture ones.
  • Understand my visitors: I want to see how people use my site so I can improve it over time.
  • Stay secure: If something fishy happens (like an unauthorized login attempt), stuff like IP addresses helps me keep the site safe.

4. Do I Share Information with Anyone?

I’m not in the business of selling your info—never will be. But since I use third-party tools, some sharing happens. Here’s how:

  • With Google: Google Analytics and AdSense are Google’s tools, so they get data like your browsing habits or cookie info to do their jobs.
  • Google’s ad partners: AdSense might pass along non-personal stuff (like your interests) to ad companies so they can pick the right ads.
  • Service providers: I might use third-party hosting or security services, and they could access info to help me run the site. Don’t worry—they’re only allowed to use it for that.

If the law comes knocking (like with a court order), I’d have to share info, but that’s super rare.

5. How I Protect Your Information

I take your info’s security seriously. Even though I don’t grab personal details directly, here’s what I do:

  • Security tech: I use encryption and other tools to block outsiders from sneaking into the system.
  • Limited access: Only I (and sometimes trusted service providers) can see the data, and it’s locked down tight.
  • Keeping watch: I regularly check the site to catch any problems fast.

That said, no system’s 100% bulletproof. I’ll do my best, and if something goes wrong, I’ll let you know ASAP.
